Four Corners Traverse

4/13/2013
Gretchen & Ben
Elevation gain/loss ~6000/6000
Distance ~8 miles
Elevation from ~600 - 4000 Time ~ 7 hrs

Starting at the Johnson Pass Trailhead heading up Pete's South then to Pete's North followed by Lips and “Corner Pocket” on Cornbisquit (Corner Pocket is the only named couloir that I am aware of on the trip).

Looking south from Pete's South into Johnson Pass (left) and Lynx Creek

Looking north through Turnagain Pass with the Chugach Mountains in the distance

Gretchen eyes up the first run

and it is good!

Our tracks out. I triggered a shallow ~4" wind slab (center of photo) as we had strong north winds the day before.
Lots of sluff as the cold temps are still holding.

The upper portion of the south faces were still firm and easier to boot than skin.

After hopping around for a bit I managed to dislodge a hard slab, that fortunately broke directly under my skis as opposed to above me.

Particularly since I would have gotten bounced off a few rocks before being spit out in the fan.

The snow skied great and we headed over to Lips for corner No. 3. Looking back the crown was lit up nicely.

Looking down the third door. We then skinned across to the skin track (yeah we don't have to break trail - hadn't seen anyone else all day) in Gold Pan.

Lips skied great

The south facing Gold Pan is starting to let loose! Not a place to hang out long.

Looking back from whence we came.

Last run was the north side of Cornbisquit.

It was the only run with tracks in it, but still skied great. Then it was a long ski out to the road and some walking down the highway where we were fortunate enough to be picked up by some friends.
